Born in Reggio Emilia in 1988 and educated at the University of Parma, where I obtained a BEng and a MEng degrees in Computer Engineering (both summa cum laude) working on robotics and artificial intelligence.

Currently I live in Colchester (UK) where I am a PhD Student (3rd year) and a part-time Research Officer in the Brain Computer Interfaces (BCI) Lab at University of Essex. I work in the area of brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), devices that convert userâ€™s brain activity into commands. In particular, I apply BCIs to group decision-making, helping groups to make the best decisions via estimating the confidence of each group's member in each decision through the neural signals. My main research interests are brain-computer interfaces, decision making, machine learning and neuroscience.

I am also employed as a Graduate Laboratory Assistant in various modules (see teaching).

In the past I participated (with the robotics team of the University of Parma) to the Sick Robot Day 2012, an international robotics competition opened to universities: our team got the first place. We wrote a paper about this experience that I presented in the 4th International Conference on Robotics in Education in 2013.

In 2015 I served as Programme Chair for the 7th Computer Science and Electronic Engineering Conference (CEEC'15). I am a graduate student member of IEEE and EMBS.
