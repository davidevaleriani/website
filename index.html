Born in Reggio Emilia (Italy) in 1988, living in Colchester (UK).

PhD Student (3rd year) in the Brain Computer Interfaces (BCI) Lab at University of Essex. My research is about group decision making and collaborative BCIs: in other words, developing a multiple BCIs system that help the users in a group to take the best decision.

In the past I obtained a BSc degree (Laurea) in 2010 and an MSc (Laurea magistrale) in 2013 (both summa cum laude) in Computer Engineering from the University of Parma (Italy). I focused my MSc project in developing a 3D perception system for mobile robots that I have also described in one paper. In the last year of my Master degree, I also developed a new method to automatically segment the hippocampus in histological images that can aid the detection of brain diseases. Moreover, I participated with the robotics team of the University of Parma to the Sick Robot Day 2012, an international mobile robotics competition opened to universities: our team got the first place. We wrote a paper about this experience that I presented in the 4th International Conference on Robotics in Education in 2013.

I am a graduate student member of IEEE and EMBS.
