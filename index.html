New paper published in PNAS! We developed a deep learning platform, DystoniaNet, to make automatic diagnosis of dystonia from raw structural MRI in a fraction of a second.

In my research, I develop neurotechnologies for human enhancement.

I combine machine learning and neuroimaging to develop:

  • collaborative brain-computer interfaces to improve group decision making
  • automatic diagnostic tools of dystonia
  • novel rehabilitation devices based on neurofeedback
